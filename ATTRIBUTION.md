## Intellectual debts

This work draws on, extends, or argues with the following. None of these sources endorse this work; any misreadings are mine.

- **Chad Fowler** — *Phoenix Architecture*, from which the durable/ephemeral split and the idea of regeneration from durable specs are adapted and applied to individuals rather than servers.
- **Christopher Alexander** — *A Pattern Language*, *The Timeless Way of Building*, and *The Nature of Order*. The concepts of patterns, unfolding, and the inhabitant-as-authority are drawn from his work. The shift to just-in-time unfolding (as opposed to Alexander's batched, physicality-constrained unfolding) is a departure.
- **Geoffrey Litt, Ink & Switch, and the local-first / malleable software community** — for the substrate conditions that make small, reversible, individually-authored unfoldings possible.
- **Konstantin Stanislavski** — origin of the term *throughline* (*сквозное действие*), used here in a generalized sense beyond theater.
- **Foundation Capital (Jaya Gupta, Ashu Garg) and the broader context graphs discussion** — for the insight that decision traces are the durable layer that makes AI useful. This work takes that insight and inverts its locus: from institutional memory owned by enterprises to personal memory owned by individuals.
- **The Ship of Theseus** and its many cross-cultural cousins — for the underlying pattern of persistence through incremental replacement.
